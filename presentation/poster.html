Introduction

Glioblastoma (GBM) is the most aggressive primary brain tumour with median OS of 14–16 months. Standard clinical imaging uses RANO criteria — measuring contrast-enhancing volume on T1Gd MRI.

However, GBM infiltrates 2–3 cm beyond the visible enhancement. The peritumoral tissue visible on FLAIR MRI contains infiltrating cells, angiogenic signals, and immune microenvironment changes that are prognostically important but clinically ignored.

Research Question: Do quantitative radiomic features from peritumoral tissue provide independent prognostic information beyond what is visible in the tumour itself?

Conclusions
  • Intratumoral radiomics: AUC 0.710 vs volume-only 0.627 (+13%)
  • Peritumoral Ring 5mm: AUC 0.670 vs tumor-only 0.562 (+19%)
  • T1Gd rings outperform FLAIR rings — vascular permeability signal
  • Sparse logistic regression wins with peritumoral features
  • SHAP XAI provides biologically interpretable feature rankings

The tissue surrounding the visible tumour boundary carries more discriminative signal than the tumour itself — validating the "beyond visible segmentation" hypothesis.
